How to Set Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Last updated: March 2026 · Audience: All Users

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security to your account. Once enabled, you'll enter a verification code from your phone each time you log in.

Before You Start

You'll need a mobile authenticator app installed on your phone. We support Google Authenticator, Authy, and Microsoft Authenticator. If you don't have one, download it from the App Store or Google Play before continuing.

Step 1: Open Security Settings

Go to Settings → Security → Two-Factor Authentication and click Enable 2FA.

Step 2: Scan the QR Code

Open your authenticator app, tap the + button, and scan the QR code shown on screen. Your app will generate a 6-digit verification code.

Step 3: Enter the Verification Code

Type the 6-digit code from your authenticator app into the confirmation field and click Verify. The code refreshes every 30 seconds — enter the current one.

Step 4: Save Your Recovery Codes

After verification, you'll see a set of one-time recovery codes. Save these somewhere safe — they're the only way to access your account if you lose your phone.

Important: Recovery codes can only be viewed once. If you lose them, you'll need to contact support to regain access to your account.

What Happens Next

The next time you log in, you'll enter your password followed by the 6-digit code from your authenticator app. To disable 2FA later, return to Settings → Security and click Disable 2FA.
